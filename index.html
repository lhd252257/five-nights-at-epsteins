Welcome to Five Nights at Epstein's

Know Your Enemies in Five Nights at Epstein's

Understanding the unique behaviors and attack patterns of each enemy in Five Nights at Epstein's is absolutely crucial for survival. Each adversary presents distinct challenges requiring different defensive strategies:

The primary antagonist moves rapidly between rooms and can suddenly appear at your window without warning. Epstein requires constant camera monitoring and immediate response when detected. Slow reactions or delayed defensive measures often result in instant defeat. His unpredictable movement patterns make him the most dangerous threat throughout all five nights.

FC

This enemy begins near the back rooms and responds effectively to audio lure distractions. FC can be managed through strategic sound deployment, but ignoring this threat during multitasking moments can quickly overwhelm your defenses. Always verify FC's position after using audio distractions to ensure the lure was effective.

Trump

This adversary attacks exclusively through the vent systems and signals his approach with distinctive loud banging sounds. Quick vent closures are absolutely crucial to prevent oxygen loss and avoid fatal attacks. Never ignore vent audio cues, as Trump can breach your defenses faster than any other enemy.

Stephen Hawking

Uniquely positioned at a fixed camera location, this enemy completely ignores audio lures and must be dealt with using manual system intervention. Failing to address his presence can lead to sudden, unexpected movement elsewhere in the facility. Monitor his camera feed regularly and respond immediately when his behavior changes.
